Problem

Typically, persons are forced to adjust to life with all of its nuisances, including distractions from external noise within their environment that causes an involuntary split of their attention. Our aim is to free persons from having to mentally “block out” sounds surrounding them by doing so for them, all done while still allowing them to enjoy their favourite songs at any volume.

Mission

SoundOut is an active noise cancellation software­-based system which uses the noise cancelling technique of creating signals in antiphase with that of the noise to cancel this noise from what the user hears through their headphones. This report will carry you through the methods with which noise cancellation can be achieved using only the built in system components on a regular modern personal computer, and our software.

Challenge

Typically, noise cancelling headphones achieve noise cancellation using hardware. These headphones are specially designed and usually have two microphones close to each ear that pick up the ambient noise that needs to be inverted. These headphones have been around from as far back as the 1950s. The best noise cancelling headphone that is currently available is the Bose Quiet Comfort at a cost of US$300. These headphones aren't just expensive but they also incorporate the use of a battery which means that they cannot be used for the same duration that you could use a standard headphone coupled with our software implementation.

How we will do it

Humans tend to be better at discerning low frequency sounds than higher frequency sounds. As a result, our application needed to prioritise the existence of low frequencies in a sample. The Bark scale was used for this purpose. The Bark scale is essentially a logarithmic scale at frequencies above 500Hz and becomes more and more linear as we analyse frequencies below 500Hz. We used this scale to lessen the number of frequencies that we would have to analyse since we needed to ensure that the processing delay was minimal.
